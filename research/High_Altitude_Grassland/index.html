Carbon and Ecohydrological Dynamics of High Altitude Grasslands

Jan 01, 2026

High-altitude Himalayan grasslands are fragile yet vital ecosystems that regulate regional climate, water availability, and carbon storage, while sustaining biodiversity and pastoral livelihoods. Their sensitivity to environmental change stems from extreme elevation, harsh climatic conditions, and limited ecological resilience, making them particularly vulnerable to climate change and human pressures. Systematic monitoring of carbon and hydrological fluxes is therefore essential: carbon fluxes reveal whether these landscapes act as sinks or sources of greenhouse gases, shaping global carbon budgets and climate feedbacks, while hydrological fluxes illuminate soil moisture dynamics, evapotranspiration, and downstream water supply critical to river systems and communities. By analyzing ecohydrological variability, scientists gain insights into ecosystem functioning, resilience, and sustainability, advancing both fundamental understanding and informing conservation strategies and policy frameworks aimed at safeguarding these unique high-altitude environments for the future.
