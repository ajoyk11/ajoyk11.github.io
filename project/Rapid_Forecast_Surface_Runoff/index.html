Coupling atmospheric and hydrodynamic model for rapid forecast of surface runoff

Avijnan Das, Nilay Dey, Ajoy Karmakar, Sayantan Majumder
Nov 30, 2024 

The coupling between the atmospheric model (WRF–ARW) and the hydrodynamic model (HEC–RAS) proved highly effective. By integrating meteorological inputs from Metgrid, static boundary conditions from Geogrid, and terrain information from the Digital Elevation Model (DEM), the system was able to simulate rainfall forecasts with remarkable accuracy. These forecasts were then combined with land use/land cover (LULC) data and SCS curve number–based infiltration parameters to drive the hydrodynamic simulations.

As a result, the coupled framework successfully predicted the devastating flood that occurred in the Chaliyar river basin, Kerala, between 10–15 August 2018, a full 15 days in advance. This achievement highlights the robustness of the methodology outlined in the flowchart.

The second major objective was to develop an Earth Engine–based interactive mapping platform. This tool allows users to visualize the spatial extent of flood inundation in a dynamic and user-friendly way. By linking the forecast outputs with historical flood maps and cloud-based visualization, the system provides an interactive interface where flood spread areas can be explored in detail.

At present, the entire forecasting and visualization pipeline can be updated on a 15-day cycle, ensuring that both the hydrological predictions and the interactive maps remain current and relevant for decision-making and disaster preparedness.
